Pork Sinigang

Pork Sinigang, or sinigang na baboy, is a quintessential Filipino soup or stew defined by its savory, sour broth and packed with tender pork and a variety of vegetables. It is a beloved comfort food, particularly popular during colder weather, and is typically served with steaming white rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. pork belly sliced into cubes
  • 2 to 3 cups kangkong leaves
  • 3 pieces long green chili pepper siling pansigang
  • 2 pieces tomato quartered
  • 1 cup sliced daikon radish labanos
  • 10 to 12 pieces snake beans cut in 2 inch length pieces
  • 1 piece onion quartered
  • 15 pieces okra
  • 2 pieces taro (gabi) peeled and cut in half
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 packs Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix Original (20g pack)
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce patis
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Pour water into a cooking pot. Let it boil
  2. Add onion and tomato. Boil for 5 to 8 minutes.
  3. Put the pork in the cooking pot. Boil for 25 minutes.
  4. Add the taro. Cover and continue to boil for 30 minutes.
  5. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc mix, fish sauce, and ground black pepper; stir.
  6. Put the daikon radish in. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  7. Add the chili peppers, okra, and snake beans. Cook for 7 minutes.
  8. Add the kangkong. Cover the cooking pot and turn off heat. Let it stay covered for 5 minutes.
  9. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve with a small bowl with patis and spicy chili.
  10. Share and enjoy!
